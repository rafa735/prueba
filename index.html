50th Aniversary of Kamen Rider

Kamen Rider is celebrating its 50th anniversary with a new movie, a new television show and for the first time a new anime.

Toei Company's Kamen Rider turns 50 this year, and to celebrate, the company has unveiled a trio of new projects. The projects include Shin Kame Rider, a live-action film from Shin Godzilla co-director Anno Hideaki opening in March 2023; Kamen Rider Black Sun, a reboot of the 1980s TV series from director Shiraishi Kazuya arriving in spring 2022; and Fuuto PI, an anime series from Funimation based on the manga from Sanjo Riku and Sato Masaki arriving in summer 2022.

During the Kamen Rider 50th Anniversary press conference, Toei Productions revealed the Shin Kamen Rider movie which will be directed and written by Hideaki Anno, known for his work in Neon Genesis Evangelion, Shin Godzilla, and the upcoming Shin Ultraman movie.

Toei has announced a reboot to the 1987 Kamen Rider Black titled Kamen Rider Black Sun.The series will be aimed at older fans of the Kamen Rider series. The original Kamen Rider Black was the only series to have a direct sequel with Kamen Rider Black RX. The show’s logo is designed by calligrapher Souun Takeda while Kazuya Shiraishi will direct the series. Shiraishi is known for his work on Blood of the Wolves, a critically acclaimed crime drama movie.

For Toei’s first project announcement celebrating the Kamen Rider franchise’s 50th anniversary, an anime adaptation of Kamen Rider W‘s sequel manga, Fuuto PI, will be slated to release in summer 2022. Funimation also announced the anime will be streaming on their platform for North America.
